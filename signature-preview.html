How to add this in Gmail

  1. Click and drag your cursor over the signature you want — from the logo image to the last link. (Or triple-click inside, then expand the selection if needed.)
  2. Press Ctrl+C (Windows) or Cmd+C (Mac) to copy.
  3. Open Gmail → gear icon ⚙ → See all settings.
  4. In the General tab, scroll down to Signature.
  5. Click Create new, name it (e.g., "FieldPoint"), then paste with Ctrl+V / Cmd+V into the signature editor box.
  6. Below the signature editor, set "Signature defaults" to use the new signature for both new emails and replies.
  7. Scroll to the very bottom of the page and click Save Changes.

Tip: compose a test email to yourself to confirm the logo and links render correctly. If the logo doesn't show, your domain's SSL cert just needs another few seconds to be globally cached.
